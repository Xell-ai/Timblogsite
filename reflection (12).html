the things we do for love

'love' is great. it makes us do greater things.
'love' is irrational. it makes us act like we've never done before, that we're even suprised when we reflect on it.
'love' is impractical, because what do you mean—you're going to wait countless hours just to see that smile?
you're going to wait on a long line, feel groggy after hours of commute just to be with that person?
it creates patience. we're quite aware that all of these things are pointless, but we're still going to do them. knowing that it's for the person we love.
it's also a sacrifice. we wouldn't think much about our effort or even point it out—cancelling all your plans just to make that person feel seen and loved. like going your way out to buy a big ass cake even if it cost you a part of your savings in january. you weren't going to care how much would it cost you as long as we see the person happy albeit you are stingy.
we would never see things as inconveniences if it's for the convenience of the other person. we become selfless beings, wearing down our own interests to care for the well-being of someone. the urge of sacrifice becomes natural.

those are the things we do for love.
