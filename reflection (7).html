loser enough

"losers got nothing to lose" they say.

im writing these with a pretty banged left thumb that i just accidentally hit with a hammer—whilst bookbinding.

i'd like to say that i've been dissapointed a lot trying to achieve what's perfect. there's is never a thing or moment in my life that i've done things perfectly. it would always be flawed, imbalanced and imperfect. I'm not saying this out of pity, but of realization.

Instead of pitying myself for not achieving what's perfect, i started to appreciate the beauty of perfect imperfections of the things in life. that's what make us human, bound to make mistake and sometimes bound to fail other people's expectations.

that's the concept im trying to propose, the beauty of perfect imperfections; you can never do things perfect but you can always try your best to do what you got to do..

Your 'best' might not yet be someone else's best. So just give it your all, everytime you do an act, even if its something small. You always gotta give it your all because you'll never know when's the next time. Do your best as if it's your last.

You're not a ''try-hard" if you felt satisfied over your work or progress. You either die trying or dying by not going out of the box.

It's not even about the goal but the journey or path that shapes you and make your life worth living.
