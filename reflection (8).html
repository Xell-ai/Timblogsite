To be loved is to be changed

when i was a kid, i used to have a teddy bear. I call it "bearbear" or "berber" because its head is shaped liked a bear, with two black round eyes; and it has no mouth, just a protruding circle, a triangle nose, and fuzzy white skin, with a kid uniform that is sewed into its part. it was the best companion my mother had given me. i used to to bite and drool on its ears when im trying to sleep from my mom's beating. even now, i can still remember that stuff toy. And even having dreams about it from time to time.

i can still perfectly recall the details of its shape and design as if i still had it. i have lost that teddy and probably was thrown away because it was soaked in floodwater. that wasn't just a stuff toy for me, it had an impact to my childhood. as time goes by, its insides were sometimes bursting out and mother had to mend it for me, even giving it a new shirt. when it's eyes was snucked out, i tried to repair it myself buy putting a hot glue on it—even though that wasn't the right operation to perform for my teddy. i used to look to look up the fictional character "Mr. Bean" about it because we almost had the same thing and Mr. Bean carries his teddy everywhere he goes. i had done everything to keep it. that toy aged with me, got worn out and eventually thrown away; which saddened me when i finally had to let go of it.
