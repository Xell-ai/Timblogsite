people are like pins

enamel pins comes in different sizes
they also come in different designs and vibrance.
i love pins.
they have become a part of me—my way to show my artistic expression. i have even made my own. but i have learned something bigger than that.

it's not about how much every pins in my bag costs me. like all of it that are attached to my bag were literally my money, as i have spent so much for those.

i love and cherish each of it. i am attached to every piece. all of it are important to me.

so losing them would simply mean losing something important for me.
< pins are like people. you could lose them.
some stays and some could be lost. i have lost so many pins over a year;and i get so sentimental when losing them. because i don't buy extras, i always do 1:1. they're irreplaceable, so when they're gone, i just contemplate and accepts what happened.

it could be my fault—maybe not keeping enough watch to my connections. so, the next time, i get one, i put in extra security of another safety pin to hook it, hoping it wont get lost anymore.

i am still losing my pins overtime and unknowingly. that's another thing that i've learned about, it is learning to let go.
it is inevitable, that im going to lose them, whether i like it or not. but knowing that it's a part of our lives, gives me a strange kind of peace. that everything is temporary but change never is.
